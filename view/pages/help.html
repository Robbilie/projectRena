Home

There is not much you could possibly need help with on this page.
It is just a short introduction of what this page is and what you can find here, including the small tour.

Profile

The Profile page lists basic information about your currently logged in character.

ACTIVE CHARACTER
At the top you will find your characters portrait, a corporation and eventually an alliance logo aswell as the appropriate names right next to it.
Next to it (or below, depending on your screen width) there is a list of the groups you are currently member of. This incudes your corporation and alliance group but also the ones you have been assigned by a CEO/Director.

ALL CHARACTERS / ADD CHARACTER
The character list shows you which characters are connected to your alliance service user. Each character, displayed with alliance, corporation logo and character portrait and character name, has a floating × right next to it (excluding the currently active character).
Pressing the × icon will immediatly remove the character from your user.
To readd or add annother character, you press the "Login with EVE SSO" button like you did on the initial login.
While beeing logged in and pressing that button, the character you then log in with will be added to your user and you can freely switch between them.
Clicking a characters name from the already mentioned list will make this character the currently active one, you will switch to this character.

SERVICES
The services section includes various modifications (mainly passwords), the abbility to add and remove jabber, mumble and teamspeak accounts.
Once logged in you gain the abbility to login to the Jabber server with the listed JID once you set a password initially. You can change this in the texfield whenever you want.
Adding works with the textfield, removing works like the characterlist, pressing the ×.

Notifications

The page is mainly split up into two sections, Tasks & Notifications, which can be expanded on click. Additionally you can tweak some settings in the menu at the lower left.
This allows you to 'Mark All as Read' and toggle each notification/task type on or off.

Tasks
Tasks are some kind of special notifications. The main difference is that you usually can interact with them, complete, fail and progress them and the time left is shown instead of a timestamp.
Additionally, the sort order is different. The Task that has to be completed next shows on top while tasks that have more time left show lower in the list.

Notifications
Notifications are the same as you know them from in the game. Eventually there are more different types.
Newest Notfications are at the top, older ones at the bottom.
Notifications have a timestamp at the top to show when they were received/created.

Mails

On this page you will find the mails received by you, your corp or your alliance. Mails are opened/expanded by clicking on them. The following sidebar items define what is shown.

All
This tab shows you all mails together, including personal, corporation and alliance mails.

Personal
Here you will find the mails personally adressed to the currently logged in character.

Corporation
Mails sent to your corporation will appear in this tab.

Alliance
Mails sent to your alliance will appear in this tab.

Intel

The Intel Tool is an essential part of the alliance services. It interactively shows you in realtime information about a certain system or a region, what neutral characters are located where.
Intel data is generated by users pasting the member list of the local into the text area in the settings and it magically feeds the tool.
There are two views, the system and the region view. Settings can be tweaked in the lower left.

System
The System View shows you the state of the intel tool at the top. There are different states, offline means either it was unable to initialize (reload) or you dont have the required permissions to read Intel.
Under the status the system name and region name are displayed.
If there are neutrals in the surrounding systems, a list of how many and where is displayed below.
The main content is filled with a list of neutral characters or a list of alliances/corporations if the character numer exceeds a certain limit.
Clicking on a neutral character will bring up a textfield to enter additional data like the ship type or visibility. Remember that the last set info will be shown to everyone, submitted by pressing the button next to the text field.
When a neutral is 2 systems out, the page will turn yellow saying "Wake up", a neutral one system away will make it turn orange, "Attention" and a neutral in the same system will make it turn red, "Warning".

Region
The Region view shows you a map that you are probably familar with.
A map passed to the tool from Dotlan's Evemaps is displayed with blank nodes and only system names.
When neutrals are registered in a system on this map the system glows up in orange. Clicking on a system will switch to the system view for that system.

Settings
The settings in the lower left corner are essential for the use of the tool. They not only offer you configuration but are also where you paste the local member list.
To track your character in the Ingame Browser (IGB) you need to grant trust to the website, you do this by pressing the lower left button in the settings popup. Intel should immediatly work, refreshing the page isnt a bad idea though.
You can either choose to track your character (use in game) or to watch a special system/paste data for a different system than the current one.
By default you track the character, this is synchronized between the IGB open window and any other open instances of the intel tool. If you choose to not track the character an additional field for the system you want to paste data for appears.
When typing the system name, it will offer you auto complete suggestions, once a system name is choosen the view will switch to that system.
When you copy the member list from the game to the text field a popup is triggered on the successfull parsing of the data you entered.
